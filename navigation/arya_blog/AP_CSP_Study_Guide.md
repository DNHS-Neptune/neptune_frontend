## 1.2: Program Function and Purpose (50%)

**Weakness**: Understanding what a program is meant to do.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll read short code snippets and write a one-sentence explanation of their purpose.
- I’ll focus on identifying the input, output, and intended function of small programs.

---

## 1.4: Identifying and Correcting Errors (60%)

**Weakness**: Spotting logic or syntax errors in code.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll practice debugging by tracing code step by step on paper.
- I’ll do more “find the bug” exercises where I correct faulty logic or missing syntax.

---

## 2.1: Binary Numbers (71%)

**Weakness**: Occasional mistakes in binary conversions or understanding.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll practice converting between binary and decimal until I’m confident.
- I’ll review how binary is used to represent data and perform simple binary arithmetic.

---

## 2.4: Using Programs with Data (50%)

**Weakness**: Applying data in meaningful ways within a program.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll write programs that filter or sort data from a list or simple CSV.
- I’ll focus on identifying how to extract useful information from structured data.

---

## 3.10: Lists (40%)

**Weakness**: Working with lists—indexing, updating, looping through data.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll create list-based problems like finding averages or removing certain values.
- I’ll practice with loops that modify or search lists, and review built-in list methods.

---

## 3.17: Algorithmic Efficiency (0%)

**Weakness**: Understanding how to evaluate the efficiency of code.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll compare different algorithms for the same task to see which is faster or simpler.
- I’ll get familiar with what makes one algorithm more efficient than another, especially when dealing with large inputs.

---

## 4.3: Parallel and Distributed Computing (0%)

**Weakness**: How multiple computers can work together to solve problems.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll review real-life examples of parallel and distributed computing (like internet searches).
- I’ll learn how tasks are split up and what benefits this brings in terms of speed and scalability.

---

## 5.1: Beneficial and Harmful Effects (50%)

**Weakness**: Evaluating both sides of how computing impacts society.

**How I’ll Study**:
- I’ll think of examples where technology had both positive and negative effects (e.g., social media, AI).
- I’ll practice writing short reflections or arguments about how technology affects daily life, privacy, and ethics.
